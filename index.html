Standards

International carbon standards

CarbonPocket follows globally recognized frameworks to keep carbon accounting accurate, auditable, and transparent.

📋

GHG Protocol — Scope Approach

The world's most widely used GHG accounting framework. CarbonPocket adapts the Scope logic for individuals: Scope 1 (direct emissions — cooking gas, vehicle fuel), Scope 2 (electricity), Scope 3 (food, clothing, electronics, flights).

WRI & WBCSD
📊

IEA + DEFRA — Emission Factors

Electricity emission factors for 96 countries from the International Energy Agency (IEA 2025) combined with DEFRA/EPA transport factors for cars, buses, trains, motorcycles, flights, and more. All validated by peer-reviewed government sources.

IEA 2025
🥩

Poore & Nemecek — Food Footprint

Published in Science (2018), this landmark peer-reviewed study provides emission factors for beef, chicken, pork, fish, dairy, grains, legumes, fruits, and beverages. It is the global reference for dietary carbon footprint.

Science 2018
🏠

IPCC + EPA — Household & Consumption

Official IPCC Guidelines (2006 with 2019 Refinement) for household energy and waste, plus EPA/DEFRA factors for clothing, electronics, pets, accommodation, streaming, and delivery — covering 22+ consumer categories.

IPCC & EPA

📊 Data sources

All emission factors in CarbonPocket are sourced from the International Energy Agency (IEA), DEFRA (UK Government), EPA (US Environmental Protection Agency), ICAO (International Civil Aviation Organization), and the scientific study Poore & Nemecek (2018). These references cover electricity grids in 96 countries, transport modes, food products, household energy, and consumer goods — making individual carbon accounting accurate and globally consistent.
